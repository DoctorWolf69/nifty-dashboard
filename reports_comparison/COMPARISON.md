## How to read

- **B takes more trades** because positions exit sooner on deteriorating evidence,
  freeing the single-position book (`MAX_OPEN_SIGNALS = 1`) for the next setup.
- **B loses roughly half as much** (−₹769 vs −₹1,741) and has a higher profit factor
  (0.89 vs 0.72) on this one day — the dynamic exits cut losers earlier and stopped
  waiting on a fixed target.
- **No `TARGET_HIT` exits in B** — the fixed profit target was removed; the catastrophic
  stop (`STOP_HIT` at entry×0.70) is retained but did not trigger this day.
- The **avg MFE +4.14% vs ~0% captured / 4.46% given back** signals exits are still firing
  somewhat late relative to the peak — the conviction-fade / confirmation thresholds
  (`CONVICTION_FADE_DROP`, `CONVICTION_FADE_STREAK`, `CONFIRMATION_LOST_MIN` in
  `nifty/dashboard/state.py`) are first proposals and are the natural next tuning knobs.

> Single day, un-tuned thresholds — directional evidence only, not a validated result.
> Both runs use the identical archived ticks; the only difference is the trade-management code.
